Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most common reason for an extra charge on a dumpster rental?

The most common reasons are overloading the dumpster past the weight limit included in your quote or disposing of prohibited items like tires, hazardous waste, or electronics. We are upfront about all potential charges and will help you choose a roll off dumpster size in Salt Lake City, UT to avoid them.

Can I put the dumpster on the street?

In most municipalities, placing a roll off dumpster on a public street requires a permit. We recommend checking with your local city or town hall before scheduling your delivery if you cannot place it on your own property.

What if I fill my dumpster and my project isn't finished?

No problem at all. Just give us a call, and we can schedule what’s called a "dump and return." We will come pick up the full dumpster and deliver a fresh, empty one so your project doesn't lose momentum.

What is the difference between construction debris and household junk?

Construction debris includes materials like wood, drywall, roofing, and siding. Household junk is more general, like old furniture, clothes, and clutter. Both can go in our dumpsters, but heavy debris like concrete needs its own dedicated concrete roll off dumpster in Salt Lake City, UT.

How far in advance do I need to order my dumpster?

We recommend ordering your roll off rental at least 24-48 hours in advance. For our emergency storm cleanup service, we do our best to provide same-day service whenever possible.

Salt Lake City, UT Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Salt Lake City, often shortened to Salt Lake or SLC, is the capital and most populous city of the U.S. state of Utah. It is the seat of Salt Lake County, the most populous county in the state. With a population of 200,133 in 2020, it is the 117th most populous city in the United States, the city is the core of the Salt Lake City metropolitan area, which had a population of 1,257,936 at the 2020 census. Salt Lake City is further situated within a larger metropolis known as the Salt Lake City–Ogden–Provo Combined Statistical Area, a corridor of contiguous urban and suburban development stretched along a 120-mile (190 km) segment of the Wasatch Front, comprising a population of 2,746,164 (as of 2021 estimates), making it the 22nd largest in the nation. It is also the central core of the larger of only two major urban areas located within the Great Basin (the other being Reno, Nevada).
